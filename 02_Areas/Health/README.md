## Overview & Principles

Holistic health and wellness management encompassing physical fitness, mental health, and work-life balance. Focus on maintaining optimal performance during career transition and establishing sustainable long-term health practices.

## Current Focus Areas

### Q1 2025 Goals
- [ ] Establish consistent exercise routine (5x/week minimum)
- [ ] Maintain work-life balance during intensive job search
- [ ] Develop stress management strategies for interview process
- [ ] Create sustainable daily routines that support productivity

### Key Responsibilities
- **Physical Fitness** - Regular exercise, nutrition, and health monitoring
- **Mental Health** - Stress management, emotional well-being, mindfulness
- **Work-Life Balance** - Boundaries between work, learning, and personal time
- **Energy Management** - Optimizing daily routines for sustained high performance

## Success Metrics

- **Fitness** - Consistent exercise frequency, strength/endurance improvements
- **Mental Health** - Stress levels, sleep quality, mood stability
- **Balance** - Time allocation across work, learning, relationships, rest
- **Energy** - Sustained productivity without burnout

## Maintenance Practices

### Daily
- Physical activity (30+ minutes)
- Mindfulness or meditation practice
- Adequate sleep (7-8 hours)
- Healthy nutrition choices

### Weekly
- Fitness routine assessment
- Work-life balance review
- Stress level and coping strategy evaluation
- Social connection and relationship nurturing

### Monthly
- Health metrics review (weight, fitness progress)
- Mental health assessment
- Routine optimization based on what's working
- Medical/dental check-ups as needed

### Quarterly
- Comprehensive health and wellness review
- Major routine adjustments
- Professional health consultations
- Long-term health goal setting

## Key Principles

1. **Consistency Over Perfection** - Small daily habits compound
2. **Holistic Approach** - Physical, mental, and social health interconnect
3. **Sustainable Practices** - Build routines that last long-term
4. **Stress as Fuel** - Use challenge as motivation, not burnout source

## Health During Career Transition

1. **Maintain Routines** - Stability in health practices during change
2. **Manage Stress** - Interview anxiety, rejection resilience
3. **Energy Optimization** - Peak performance for networking and interviews
4. **Recovery Planning** - Rest and restoration between intense periods

## Related Areas

- **Career-Development** - Energy and mental clarity for professional growth
- **Finance** - Healthcare costs and planning
- **Professional-Network** - Social connections support mental health

*Physical and mental health are the foundation that enables all other life areas to thrive.*
